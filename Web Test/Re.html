Words[edit]

  • re (re:), a word meaning "regarding, with reference to".
  • 're, a contracted form of are, part of the English verb to be
  • Re, 'in the matter of...'; see the list of Latin phrases and In re
  • RE: or Re:, an email subject abbreviation meaning "in reply to" derived from latin In re.
  • re means again, e.g. re-reads
  • re, a chemical descriptor, see Prochirality
  • Re (exclamation), in Greek
  • Re (kana), the Japanese syllables written れ and レ
  • Re, the ancient Egyptian god Ra

Abbreviations, acronyms and codes[edit]

  • Re, or Reinsurance, a process whereby an insurer spreads its own risks
  • RE (automobile), a 1960s Rhodesian Formula One racing car
  • RE (complexity), the set of recursively enumerable languages
  • RE (e-mail), or "Re:", followed by the subject of a previous message indicates a reply to the subject of that message. It is an abbreviation from the legal Latin 'In re' = "in the matter of" (see Fowlers Modern English Language p. 653)
  • RE, the IATA code for Stobart Air, an Irish regional airline
  • RE, meaning rare earth, one of a set of seventeen chemical elements in the periodic table
  • RE, meaning real estate, property consisting of land and the buildings on it
  • RE, meaning Reproductive endocrinologist, a doctor specializing in the field of fertility
  • RE, meaning rotary engine, an early type of internal-combustion engine
  • Royal Society of Painter-Printmakers (RE), known until 1991 as the Royal Society of Painter-Etchers and Engravers
  • Realencyclopädie der Classischen Altertumswissenschaft, commonly called the Pauly–Wissowa or simply RE, a German encyclopedia of classical scholarship
  • Recursively enumerable set (r.e.), a special set S of natural numbers
  • Relative effectiveness factor, or R.E. factor, relates an explosive's demolition power to that of TNT
  • Resident Evil, a franchise of horror-adventure video games
  • Réunion, a French island (pop. ~800,000) near Mauritius, ISO 3166 digram and FIPS PUB 10-4 territory code
    • .re, Internet country code top-level domain (ccTLD) of Réunion
  • Reuptake enhancer, a type of drug which enhances reuptake of a neurotransmitter
  • Reverse engineering, the process of taking something (a device, an electrical component, a software program, etc.) apart and analyzing its workings in detail
  • Requirements engineering during the development of computer programs
  • Rock-Eval, an apparatus for assessing the oil potential of a sedimentary rock, designed at the French Institute of Petroleum
  • Royal Engineers, a corps of the British Army
  • Religious education
  • Abbreviations of single words:
    • Re(z) or : the real part in mathematics
    • Reynolds number, in physics
    • Réaumur scale degree of temperature (°Ré)
    • Rhenium, a chemical element whose symbol is "Re"
    • Re., or Rupee, a currency in many Asian countries
