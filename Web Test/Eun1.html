Eun is an uncommon Korean family name, a single-syllable Korean given name, and an element in many two-syllable Korean given names.

As a family name[edit]

The Korean family name Eun is written with only one hanja, meaning "flourishing" (æ®·).[1] The 2000 South Korean Census found 15,657 people with this family name.[2] They traced their origins to three different bon-gwan: Yonan County, South Hwanghae and Kangeum, Kumchon County, North Hwanghae in what is today North Korea, and Haengju (í–‰ì£¼ë?™), Goyang, Gyeonggi-do in what is today South Korea.[1]

People with this family name include:

  • Eun Hee-kyung (born 1959), South Korean writer
  • Eun Ji-won (born 1978), South Korean rapper, member of Sechs Kies

In given names[edit]

There are 26 hanja with the reading Eun on the South Korean government's official list of hanja which may be used in given names; common ones are listed in the table above. The meaning differs based on the hanja which the name contains.[3] Since the 1970s, a number of given names containing this element have been popular for newborn girls in South Korea, including:

First syllable
  • Eun-kyung: 8th place in 1970[4]
  • Eun-ji: 2nd place in 1980[4]
  • Eun-ju: 6th place in 1970[4]
  • Eun-jung: 3rd place in 1970, 6th place in 1980[4]
  • Eun-seo: 10th place in 2011[5]
  • Eun-young: 5th place in 1970, 8th place in 1980[4]
Second syllable
  • Ha-eun: 7th place in 2008, 8th place in 2009 and 2011[5][6][7]
  • Ji-eun: 4th place in 1980, 2nd place in 1990[4]
  • Ye-eun: 6th place in 2008, 9th place in 2009[6][7]

Other names containing this element include:
